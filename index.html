An Analyst's Journey: Applying Quantitative Methods to Modern Challenges

By Richard T. Shen

I am an applied econometrician from Shenzhen MSU-BIT University, with a core focus on two key areas: (1) developing computationally efficient econometric tools and (2) leveraging economic theory and big data techniques to generate actionable policy insights. My work bridges these areas by introducing an easy-to-implement method to address critical policy-relevant questions, such as how we should test for the existence and extent of interference, such as peer and spillover effects.

My broader research interests span Econometrics, Labor Economics, Digital Economics, and Microeconomic Theory. This document chronicles my professional experiences and academic pursuits.
